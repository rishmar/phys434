  1. State the statistical question in words. Be very clear, and describe why you have chosen this statistical question. Restate your question in math. Be very clear.

Suppose there's a temperature measurement X. What is the probability that a measurement at 12 Kelvin within the SD 0.4, would be far away from mean than X? Suppose measurement cannot exceeds 3 $\sigma$.

  1. Restate your question in math. Be very clear.

probability of X from gaussian distribution with 12Kelvin within SD 0.4: $$P(X) = 2 \int_{|X-12|}^\infty\frac{1}{0.4}e^{\frac{-1}{2} (\frac{x}{0.4})^2}$$

Then we use quantile function: $$Q(p) = (2p-1) \sqrt{\frac{1}{2p-2p^2}}$$

Finally, if the answear is bigger than 3, we discard the answear.

We would like a '5 sigma' detection of movement. What is that distance in arcseconds?

What is the radius around the center of the image that detections outside of that radius have more than or equal to $5\sigma$?

We knoe that the magnitude of a vector of two independent Gaussians is a Rayleigh distribution, the pdf of this Rayleigh distribution is: $p(x)=\frac{x}{\sigma^2}e^{\frac{-x^2}{2\sigma^2}}$

Gaussian distributions with $\sigma = 1$, we got the cdf: $ C(r) = 1-e^{\frac{-a^2}{2}}$ and Quantile function is: $Q=\sqrt{-2\ln(1-C(r))}$

If the answear of Q is larger than 5, then it's discard.

Assume in a moon sized patch on the sky we normally have a cosmic ray rate of 1 cosmic ray per minute (arrivals are random in time). If we can observe where the moon is for 8 hours per night (not too close to the horizon) and we observe for 15 days and see 6800 cosmic rays, what is the signficance of our moon shadow detection?

Assume a poisson signal free background pdf with mu qeual to 1 cosmic ray/min, what is the significane of detecting 6800 gamma rays after 7200minutes?

$\lambda = 7200$ and $k = 6800$

We integrate pmf of our background from 0 to 6800 then convert it to sigma. The precise formula for integrattion: $P(k |\lambda) = e^{-\lambda} \sum \limits_{i=0}^k \frac{\lambda^i}{i!}$
