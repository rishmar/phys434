Higgs Classification In this project we are going to look at an inclusive search for the standard model Higgs boson in pp collisions at s = 13 TeV at the LHC using simulated data [A. Schuy]. The Higgs bosons are produced with large transverse momentum (pT) and decaying to a bottom quark-antiquark pair. The Higgs candidates could be reconstructed as large-radius jets using Calorimeters. Due to large QCD backgorund containmination, the direct 5-sigma observation of this Higgs channel is not accomplished yet[Phys. Rev. Lett. 120, 071802 (2018)]. We are going to use a set of training datasets to optimize event selections in order to enhance the discovery sensitivity. The optimal event selections will be applied to a selected pseudo-experiment data.

Both of you will use the same training samples for analysis. Each sample contains 14 features: pt', 'eta', 'phi', 'mass', 'ee2', 'ee3', 'd2', 'angularity', 't1', 't2', 't3', 't21', 't32', 'KtDeltaR' [Eur. Phys. J. C 79 (2019) 836]. You can explore different strategies for event selection optimization using training samples. The optimal event selection will be applied to pseudo-experiment data.

Understanding the data: The higgs are the signal dataset and the qcd are the background dataset. For each column: 1) pt: transverse momentum of jet 2) eta: function of angle $\theta$ along the beam axis in y-z plane 3) phi: azimuthal angle of jet in y-z plane 4) mass: mass of the jet 5) ee2: energy correlation function (ECF) ratio for 2-point correlators of the jet 6) ee3: energy correlation function (ECF) ratio for 3-point correlators of the jet 7) d2: ratio of ee3/ee2 8) angularity: how symetric the decay is, with lower angularity, the higher symetric 9) t1: average distance from jets with 1 subjet 10) t2: average distance from jets with 2 subjet 11) t3: average distance from jets with 3 subjet 12) t21: ratio of t2/t1 13) t32: ratio of t3/t2 14) KtDeltaR: radius of the jet

def Hisplots(key, title):
    plt.hist(higgs[key], bins=100, alpha=0.7, density=True, label='higgs')
    plt.hist(qcd[key], bins=100, alpha=0.7, density=True, label='qcd')
    plt.legend()
    plt.xlabel(f'{key}')
    plt.ylabel('Density')
    plt.title(title)
    plt.show()

We use histograms above to compare each feature of the higgs and the qcd of jets. As we can see, from the graph, mass, ee2, ee3, d2, angularity, t2, t3, t21, and KtDeltaR provide discrimination power between signal and background. pt, eta, and t1 provide a relative weaker discrimination power. phi and t32 provide no discrimination power.
